## Working title
**Recognising Inclusive Online Teaching (RIOT):** a UK–Brazil micro-credential credit-recognition framework between The Open University and IFES/CEFOR.
*(testar outros nomes; ≤25 palavras no formulário)*

## Purpose *(≤75 palavras — rascunho EN)*
> This exploratory partnership co-designs a credit-recognition framework between an existing Open University Level-7 inclusive online-teaching micro-credential and IFES/CEFOR's MOOC certification. It maps Brazil's regulatory barriers to micro-credential recognition, co-adapts the credential for the Brazilian context, and pilots it with women educators in the Federal Network — laying the foundations for scalable, inclusive UK–Brazil transnational education.

## The problem / context
- Brazil has **no federal framework** for recognising micro-credentials; the Carolina Bori platform recognises only full degrees; Brazil is outside the Lisbon Recognition Convention. → barreira que o edital premia mapear. (`../context/brazil/regulatory-recognition.md`)
- CEFOR runs MOOCs at scale (~296k enrolments) but without an international recognition pathway.
- The OU holds Level-7 stackable inclusive-teaching micro-credentials (HZVM884/885) and the OUVP validation mechanism. (`../context/partners/ou-iet.md`)

## What we will do (exploração, não pesquisa)
Co-design + co-adaptation + a small inclusive pilot + a recognition framework + a continuity pathway. **Não** construir/rodar um MOOC novo; **não** estudar impacto como pesquisa.

## Why this partnership (co-design paritário)
- **OU/IET:** micro-credential design, FHEQ L7 recognition, OUVP, accessibility, learning design.
- **CEFOR/IFES:** the **ADDIEM** MOOC method, scale, award-winning inclusion (CBIE 2024), national reach via CONIF.
